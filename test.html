The Experience


Recently I have been inspired by the duality of the mental and the physical.
How does a physical experience trigger a mental experience?

To explore what connects and separates the two, I have begun with the idea of discovery.
Could discovering a miracle cause a rebirth of the psyche?
I have been meditating over the infinite possibilities that could exist somewhere in the far reaches of the universe, and the impact that it would have on the mind to witness a true phenomenon that invalidated all its preceding understanding.
To the mind it would feel like the physical world had changed, but it would just be the understanding of reality that had grown.
My short film Catalyst investigates his belittling expansion of the mind to forms unfathomable to us in the present.

As I study physical and mental metamorphosis I continue to be inspired by new questions.
How are moving to a new place physically and moving to a new mentality similar?
Can the mind circumvent an idea similarly to the body physically circumventing an object in space?
And if physical migration leaves a physical footprint, does mental migration leave a mental footprint?
My short film Migration explores the expansion of one's conciousness through considering the perspectives of others.

I am also captivated by water and the idea of its surface representing a permeable layer separating the physical and mental.
Our idea of portals would theoretically allow movement through physical reality but what if portals could be doors into the mind?

Through my work I hope to create a physical experience and, in turn, a mental experience for my viewer.
My hope is that the physical action of being exposed to these questions provokes a mental reaction of new self-reflection and curiosity about the world around you.

Wes Bower
